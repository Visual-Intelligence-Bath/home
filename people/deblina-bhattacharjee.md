Deblina's research develops responsible multi-modal AI methods for visual understanding, preservation, and decision support across cultural, civic, and biomedical domains. Methodologically, Deblina works on computer vision, generative models, 3D reconstruction, multi-modal large language models, visual saliency, multitask learning, and physics-informed AI. A central strand of Deblina's work focuses on AI for arts, culture, and heritage, including the preservation, interpretation, and creative reconfiguration of visual media. In parallel, Deblina works on responsible and trustworthy digitisation of institutional archives, including United Nations archives and government record-keeping workflows, with attention to regulatory principles and public-sector accountability. Deblina also works on multi-modal physics-based AI for medical imaging, particularly cardiac blood-flow modelling. Across these strands, Deblina's broader interest is in building AI systems that are robust, inclusive, and aligned with human, institutional, and societal needs.
